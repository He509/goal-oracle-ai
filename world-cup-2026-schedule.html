🎬 The 2010 Reverse: Exactly 16 years to the day after Tshabalala's iconic strike united a nation at Johannesburg's Soccer City. Now reversed at Estadio Azteca — the first stadium in WC history to host 3 openers (1970 Pelé era, 1986 Maradona era, 2026). Aguirre managed Mexico in 2010 too. Ochoa — the only player on either roster who made BOTH 2010 + 2026 squads — was benched 16 years ago. Now 40, he chases history as the first to play 6 WCs.

🇲🇽 Mexico's Form Spike: 8 unbeaten friendlies in 2026 with a stunning 5-1 thrashing of Serbia, 1-0 over Australia, 2-0 vs Ghana. 6 clean sheets in last 8 games — a defensive turnaround that vindicates Aguirre's third stint. Unbeaten in their LAST 7 WC openers since 1994 (W5 D2). The 83,000 Azteca crowd should produce one of football's most hostile atmospheres.

🇿🇦 South Africa Under Pressure: A 4-match winless streak heading in. Broos publicly admitted recent 1-1 vs Jamaica was "not what I expected". Ranked 60th vs Mexico's 14th. Their qualification was helped by Nigeria upsetting Benin, plus a 3-point deduction for fielding an ineligible player.

🎯 Our Read: Statistical edge clearly Mexico's (0.4 conceded/match at home vs SA's 1.0 conceded away). Mexico Win to Nil plausible at 2.30. Sports Mole agrees: Mexico 2-1 South Africa. Watch for late SA consolation per their 2nd-half scoring trend.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

When is the World Cup 2026 opening match?

The opening match takes place Thursday, June 11, 2026, at 3:00 PM ET. Mexico hosts South Africa at the iconic Estadio Azteca in Mexico City.

When is the World Cup 2026 final?

The final takes place Sunday, July 19, 2026, at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey. Kick-off time will be confirmed closer to the date.

How many matches are at World Cup 2026?

104 matches in total. 72 group stage matches (12 groups of 4 teams) and 32 knockout matches (from Round of 32 through to the Final).

Where can I watch World Cup 2026?

In the US: Fox (English) and Telemundo (Spanish). In the UK: BBC and ITV. Globally: FIFA+, DAZN, and regional broadcasters.

What is the new World Cup 2026 format?

First time ever: 48 teams (up from 32) divided into 12 groups of 4. Top 2 from each group advance, plus the 8 best third-place teams. Total of 32 teams advance to the knockout stage.
