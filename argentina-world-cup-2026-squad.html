Lionel Scaloni announced Argentina's 26-man squad for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, with captain Lionel Messi set to play his record-breaking 6th World Cup. The defending champions will look to become the first team since Brazil (1958-1962) to win back-to-back World Cup titles.

17 of the 26 players who lifted the trophy in Qatar 2022 return for the title defense, including Messi, Julian Alvarez, Lautaro Martinez, Emiliano "Dibu" Martinez, Cristian Romero, and Alexis Mac Allister. "La Scaloneta" begins their Group J campaign against Algeria on June 17 in Kansas City.

👑 Messi's 6th World Cup — A Historic Record

Lionel Messi will become only the second player in history to play in 6 World Cups, joining Cristiano Ronaldo (41) and Mexican goalkeeper Guillermo Ochoa. Messi already holds the record for most World Cup appearances by any player with 26 games.
