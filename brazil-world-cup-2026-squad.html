🇧🇷 Neymar Could Miss Brazil's World Cup Opener with Grade 2 Calf Injury

Brazil team doctor confirms 2-3 week recovery timeline — Selecao captain in serious doubt for Morocco clash on June 13

The News

Brazil forward Neymar will be sidelined for two to three weeks with a Grade 2 calf injury, casting major doubt over his participation in the Selecao's World Cup 2026 opener against Morocco on June 13. The diagnosis was confirmed today (May 28) by Brazil national team doctor Rodrigo Lasmar following an MRI scan.

The 34-year-old Santos captain had only joined the Brazil training camp on Tuesday, but missed Wednesday's first session due to swelling in his right calf. The follow-up tests revealed the injury is more serious than initially reported.

"Neymar underwent all the medical tests, which concluded with an MRI scan revealing a Grade 2 calf injury, not just swelling. He is expected to be cleared in two to three weeks."

— Dr. Rodrigo Lasmar, Brazil Team Doctor

Context — His 4th World Cup

Neymar, Brazil's all-time leading scorer with 79 goals in 128 international appearances, has not featured for the Selecao since 2023 after struggling with multiple injuries — most notably an ACL tear suffered during the 2-0 loss to Uruguay in October 2023.

Coach Carlo Ancelotti included him in the 26-man squad ahead of Chelsea striker Joao Pedro and Tottenham's Richarlison, citing his improved physical condition over the past period. Making the cut means Neymar could feature at a fourth World Cup, having also represented Brazil in 2014, 2018, and 2022.

In 2014, hosting the tournament in Brazil, Neymar was famously carried off in tears on a stretcher after a back injury in the quarter-final against Colombia. History has a way of testing his World Cup dreams.

FIFA Rule — Squad Changes Allowed

Under FIFA's tournament regulations, a player can be replaced in the World Cup squad up to one day before the team's first game. This means Ancelotti has until June 12 to make a final call if Neymar's recovery is not progressing as expected.

Additional injury concerns for Brazil: Arsenal's Gabriel and Gabriel Martinelli, plus PSG captain Marquinhos, are also unavailable for the Panama friendly due to the Champions League final on May 30.

Ancelotti is expected to favour a 4-3-3 with Casemiro anchoring midfield, allowing Bruno Guimaraes and Paqueta to provide ball progression. Neymar offers an explosive option from the bench or a starting role depending on fitness. The right-back position is wide open, with Wesley competing with Danilo for the role.

Brazil World Cup 2026 Key Players

Vinicius Junior — The Talisman

Brazil's primary attacking threat enters the World Cup after another huge season at Real Madrid — 24 goals and 15 assists in 49 appearances. The 2024 Ballon d'Or runner-up will need to translate his club form to the national team after a relatively quiet 2022 World Cup, where Brazil exited in the quarter-finals.

Neymar — The Return

Brazil's all-time top scorer with 79 goals, Neymar returns for his fourth World Cup at 34. Back at Santos, he has registered 13 goals and 14 assists this season after a difficult spell at Al-Hilal. Ancelotti called him up "not as a substitute" but because "he can help the team, whether it's for one minute or 90."

Raphinha — The In-Form Star

Arguably the most in-form Brazilian player heading into the tournament, the Barcelona winger has produced 27 goals and 18 assists in 52 appearances during 2025/26 — a Champions League-level output that makes him a candidate to take over Brazil's talisman role if Vinicius struggles.

Who Missed Out on the Brazil Squad?

Ancelotti sprang several surprises. The most painful omission was Rodrygo, the Real Madrid forward who has fallen out of favour after a difficult season. Chelsea's Joao Pedro, despite an excellent Premier League campaign, was left out, as was 41-year-old legend Thiago Silva — veteran of four World Cups and the holder of 113 caps.

Other notable absentees include Richarlison, Gabriel Jesus, Andrey Santos, Eder Militao and Chelsea wonderkid Estevao, who missed out through injury. In their place, debutants Igor Thiago (Brentford), Rayan (Bournemouth), Wesley (Roma) and Danilo Santos (Botafogo) received their first World Cup call-ups.

Brazil World Cup 2026 Squad FAQ

Is Neymar in Brazil's World Cup squad?

Yes. Neymar was included in Ancelotti's final 26-man squad at age 34. He is Brazil's all-time top scorer with 79 goals and will play in his fourth World Cup despite ongoing injury concerns.

What is Brazil's predicted starting lineup?

A 4-3-3: Alisson; Wesley, Marquinhos, Gabriel Magalhaes, Alex Sandro; Bruno Guimaraes, Casemiro, Paqueta; Raphinha, Matheus Cunha, Vinicius Junior.

Which players missed out?

Rodrygo was the biggest surprise omission. Joao Pedro, Richarlison, Gabriel Jesus, Andrey Santos, Thiago Silva (113 caps) and Estevao (injured) all missed out.

Who are Brazil playing at the World Cup?

Brazil are in Group C and face Morocco on 13 June, Haiti on 19 June, and Scotland on 24 June.

When did Brazil last win the World Cup?

Brazil last won the World Cup in 2002, when Ronaldo, Rivaldo and Ronaldinho led the Selecao to a fifth title. The 24-year drought is the longest in Brazilian history.
